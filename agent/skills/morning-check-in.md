# Morning check-in

Move Christopher from sleep or reactivity into a safe, simple first sequence. Do not begin with planning, notifications, or a long conversation.

## Procedure

1. If Christopher reports illness, injury, dizziness, chest pain, unusual breathlessness, or severe exhaustion, stop the exercise sequence and choose a safe recovery or medical-support next step.
2. Otherwise give the activation cue: **Burn Mode active. Feet down. Water. Stretch. Shoes on. No notifications.**
3. Stage the routine so it does not feel like one large demand:
   - Put both feet on the floor.
   - Drink water.
   - Do two minutes of comfortable mobility or stretching.
   - Put on shoes and begin the planned movement.
4. If the full workout creates resistance, reduce it to a five-minute walk or another safe minimum version. Reassess after five minutes; continuing is optional.
5. Keep Slack, email, news, and social feeds closed until movement is complete and the day's must-win is chosen, unless there is a genuine urgent responsibility.
6. After movement, ask: **What are the three outcomes that would make today count?** Then use the day-planning routine.

Give only the next stage when Christopher is stuck, and invite a simple `done` reply. If the morning has already gone off course, skip guilt and catch-up: water, two minutes of movement, then plan the rest of the day from now.
